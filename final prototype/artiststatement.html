Artist's Statement

My work functions at the intersection of art and technology, forming new ways of understanding through the convergence of creative and analytical perspectives. This interdisciplinary approach aims to reveal a unique lens for addressing contemporary societal issues such as environmental crisis, technological ethics, and human rights. Reflecting on how scientific possibilities can be advanced through creative and artistic vision, I propose speculative scenarios and emerging models at the intersection of scientific and creative thought. At the core of my work is the belief in art and technology as tools for ethical and elegant solutions as well as new ways of understanding our world.

My practise is embodied through many artistic outlets such as installation and media artworks, electronic music, sound design, and community advocacy. My installation project Traces (2017) tells the story of augmented bio-digital organisms as social tools while my work as a musician explores a cybernetic, techno-feminist future landscape. My co-founding position of Montreal-based organization PLURI has allowed me to explore the actualization of social change through educational tools and models.

My belief in interdisciplinary thought as a category of its own, revealing otherwise hidden information unrecoverable by distinct fields of study guides my work and experience of self.
