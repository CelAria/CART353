Liquidity (2015)

50 x 60 inch ice carving
Installed at Concordia University for Art Matters Festival

The Canadian economy is heavily tied to the extortion of natural resources such as oil, minerals, and paper pulp. Intimate economic ties to natural resources have long been a point of contention for Canadian citizens and a political subject woven into Canadian politics

Liquidity was conceived in 2015, prompted by a crash in the Canadian dollar contributed to by low oil prices and the turbulent state Keystone XL discussions at the time.

The ice sculpture plots the two year relationship between the US dollar and the Canadian dollar (USD per 1 CAD) as observed from February 2013 through to February 2015.

Notions of sustainability in a resource-based economy and questions surrounding economic stability arise as the sculpture melts over the course of several weeks.
