About Celeste Pimm

Celeste Pimm (Montreal) is an interdisciplinary artist, musician, and community rights advocate enabled by technology. Her technical background spans the disciplines of fine arts, electronic music, fashion, mathematics as well as computer science with focus on conceptual development and critical approaches to creation.

Her artistic work has been exhibited both locally and internationally at the Venice Biennale (2013), NXNE Toronto (2013), and at Nuit Blanche in Montreal (2017). She is currently completing a Bachelors of Computer Science in the Computer Applications and Computation Arts Program at Concordia University in Montreal, Canada.

Celeste is the co-founder of Montreal-based Initiative PLURI which actively focuses on improving the safety and inclusivity of nightlife environments.
